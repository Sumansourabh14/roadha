A dashcam, short for dashboard camera, is one of the smartest investments you can make as a driver.

**If you own a car, buy it with your eyes closed.** It records everything that
happens on the road, providing clear evidence in case of accidents, disputes, or
traffic violations.

In India, where road conditions are unpredictable and traffic enforcement varies, dashcams offer protection, accountability, and peace of mind.

You should always have proof if something goes wrong. No matter what.

---

## Why Dashcams Are Necessary

- **Capture real-time evidence** of accidents, collisions, or road rage
- **Protect yourself legally** in case of false claims or disputes
- **Monitor driving behavior**: Useful for parents, fleet owners, or new drivers
- **Record hit-and-run incidents** or vandalism when parked
- **Promote safer driving**: Knowing you're being recorded encourages discipline

> A dashcam doesnâ€™t prevent accidents, but it protects you when they happen.

---

## Why Dashcams Matter in India

- High rate of road accidents and traffic violations
- Harrasment by traffic police and other officials
- Tackle road rage by having a proof of the scene
- Limited availability of CCTV or eyewitnesses
- Frequent insurance disputes and police reports
- Unpredictable behavior from other drivers, pedestrians, and animals
- Helps expose corruption or unfair treatment during traffic stops

> In India, a dashcam can be the difference between justice and confusion.

---

## Types of Dashcams

Dashcams come in different configurations depending on how much coverage you want. Here are the most common types:

### Single-Channel Dashcam

- Records only the **front view** through the windshield
- Ideal for basic driving footage and accident documentation
- Most affordable and easy to install

> Best for city drivers who want simple protection from front-end collisions or disputes.

---

### Dual-Channel Dashcam

- Records both **front and rear views**
- Rear camera is mounted on the rear windshield
- Captures tailgating, rear-end collisions, and parking incidents

> Highly recommended for highway driving and urban traffic where rear-end accidents are common.

---

### Triple-Channel Dashcam

- Records **front, rear, and cabin/interior views**
- Cabin camera captures driver and passenger behavior
- Useful for ride-sharing drivers, commercial vehicles, and family cars

> Offers full coverage (inside and out) for maximum accountability and security.

---

### 360-Degree Dashcam

A 360-degree dashcam uses multiple wide-angle lenses or a panoramic camera system to record **all around the vehicle** â€” front, rear, sides, and sometimes even the cabin.

#### Benefits:

- **Complete situational awareness** â€” captures incidents from any direction
- **Ideal for parking surveillance** â€” detects bumps, scratches, or vandalism
- **Useful in hit-and-run cases** â€” even if the impact comes from the side
- **Great for fleet vehicles or high-value cars** â€” maximum coverage and accountability

> In crowded Indian cities where vehicles are often parked in tight spots or surrounded by unpredictable traffic, a 360Â° dashcam offers unmatched protection.

#### Things to Consider:

- More expensive than regular dashcams
- Requires professional installation for optimal coverage
- Footage may be split across multiple camera feeds

> If you want full visibility and peace of mind, a 360-degree dashcam is the gold standard.
