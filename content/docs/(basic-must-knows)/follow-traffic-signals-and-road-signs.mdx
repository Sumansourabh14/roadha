Traffic signals and road signs are there to **guide and protect us**. When everyone follows them, roads become safer and more organized.

## Why This Lesson Matters

- Ignoring signals leads to chaos and accidents.
- Road signs help you anticipate hazards and follow rules.
- Obeying signals is a basic civic responsibility.

## What Do Traffic Lights Mean?

- **üî¥ Red = Stop**  
  You must stop your vehicle completely.

- **üü° Yellow = Wait / Slow Down**  
  Get ready to stop or move carefully.

  > The point of a yellow light is to indicate to the road users that the light is about to turn red and that they should start slowing down and stopping. Every driver is required by law to stop at a yellow light unless they are too close to the junction to do so safely.

  **Source:** [Jumped the yellow light and got a challan: Is this right?](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/auto/advice-centre/jumped-the-yellow-light-and-got-a-challan-is-this-right/articleshow/95855545.cms)

- **üü¢ Green = Go**  
  Move ahead, but only after checking the road is clear.

> üõë Always stop **behind the stop line**, not on it.
> Never jump a red light. Wait for green even if the road looks empty.

- **Flashing Yellow = Slow down before proceeding**

Some places may resort to a flashing yellow light in the wee hours when there is not much traffic on the road, especially at **night/early morning**.

**Source:** [Blinking yellow means signal is out of service](https://www.reddit.com/r/CarsIndia/comments/1oliuwi/comment/nmigb5o/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=web3x&utm_name=web3xcss&utm_term=1&utm_content=share_button)
